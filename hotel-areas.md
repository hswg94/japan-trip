# Recommended Hotel Areas — Tokyo

A few well-connected neighborhoods, each with a different vibe. All sit on the Yamanote Line or are one easy transfer from it (see [trains.md](trains.md)).

## Shinjuku
- Huge transit hub, tons of hotel options at every price point, nightlife (Golden Gai, Kabukicho), good base for day trips west (Hakone, Kawaguchiko)
- Best for: first-timers who want convenience and lots of food/shopping right outside the hotel

## Shibuya
- Younger, trendier, close to Shibuya Crossing, Harajuku, Omotesando
- Best for: shopping/fashion focus, slightly more compact than Shinjuku

## Ginza / Tokyo Station area
- Upscale, very central, walkable to Imperial Palace, close to Tsukiji/Tsukiji Outer Market
- Tokyo Station itself has direct Narita Express and Shinkansen access
- Best for: convenience for airport access + day trips via Shinkansen, higher-end stays

## Asakusa
- Traditional/older Tokyo feel, near Senso-ji temple, generally cheaper hotels
- Slightly further from the Yamanote loop but well served by subway (Ginza/Asakusa lines) and the Skyliner terminus is nearby at Ueno
- Best for: budget-conscious travelers, traditional atmosphere, easy Narita access via Keisei

## Ueno
- Similar budget-friendly feel to Asakusa, near Ueno Park/museums, Skyliner from Narita terminates here
- Best for: value + good Narita connections

# Airport → Hotel Area Transit

## From Haneda (HND)
Haneda has no direct JR trains — it's served only by the Keikyu Airport Line and the Tokyo Monorail, both of which require a transfer to reach the JR network (Keikyu → transfer at Shinagawa; Monorail → transfer at Hamamatsucho, both JR Yamanote Line stops).

| Area | Route (breakdown) | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Shinjuku | Keikyu Line to Shinagawa (~13-15 min) + transfer (~5 min) + JR Yamanote to Shinjuku (~20 min), or limousine bus direct | ~40-45 min |
| Shibuya | Keikyu Line to Shinagawa (~13-15 min) + transfer (~5 min) + JR Yamanote to Shibuya (~15 min), or limousine bus direct | ~35-40 min |
| Ginza/Tokyo Station | Tokyo Monorail to Hamamatsucho (~15 min) + transfer (~5 min) + JR to Tokyo Station (~5 min), or limousine bus | ~25-30 min |
| Asakusa | Keikyu Line direct (through-service onto Toei Asakusa Line, no transfer needed) | ~35-45 min |
| Ueno | Keikyu Line to Shinagawa (~13-15 min) + transfer (~5 min) + JR to Ueno (~25 min) | ~45 min |

## From Narita (NRT)
| Area | Route | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Shinjuku | Narita Express (N'EX) direct | ~80-90 min |
| Shibuya | Narita Express direct | ~80-90 min |
| Ginza/Tokyo Station | Narita Express direct to Tokyo Station | ~60 min |
| Asakusa | Keisei Skyliner or Keisei Access Ltd. Express to Nippori/Ueno, then subway | ~60-75 min |
| Ueno | Keisei Skyliner direct | ~45 min |

Notes:
- Limousine buses run from both airports to major hotels in each area — good option with heavy luggage, but slower than trains during traffic
- N'EX and Skyliner both require a seat reservation/ticket, bookable online or at the airport
- If choosing between areas mainly on airport convenience: Ueno/Asakusa favor Narita access, Ginza/Tokyo Station and Haneda pair well together
