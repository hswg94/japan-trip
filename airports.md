# Airports — Tokyo

Two airports serve Tokyo. Worth confirming which one each flight uses before booking, since they're on opposite sides of the city.

## Haneda (HND)
- Closer to central Tokyo (~30-40 min to most areas)
- Train options: Keikyu Line (to Shinagawa/Asakusa area) or Tokyo Monorail (to Hamamatsucho)
- Also has a limousine bus network to major hotels
- Generally preferred if available — less transit time, less hassle after a long flight
- Some international flights land late night/early morning; check last train times if arriving off-peak

### Haneda terminals & which station to use
- **Terminal 3** — international arrivals/departures (this is where nearly all international flights land). Follow signs down to the basement for both train lines:
  - Keikyu Airport Line: "Haneda Airport Terminal 3" station — it's the first airport stop coming from central Tokyo, so all trains toward Shinagawa/Asakusa stop here.
  - Tokyo Monorail: "Haneda Airport Terminal 3" station — its own separate stop, not the same station building as Keikyu (both are under/near the terminal, well signed).
- **Terminal 1 / Terminal 2** — domestic flights (ANA mainly at T1, JAL mainly at T2; some codeshares land here too, so check your boarding pass). Both terminals share one Keikyu station ("Haneda Airport Terminal 1・2") and are connected by a walkway; the Monorail instead has two separate stops, one per terminal.
- If unsure which terminal you're in, any airport signage/staff can point you to "Keikyu Line" or "Monorail" — both run every few minutes and reach Shinagawa/Hamamatsucho in ~15 min from either terminal.

## Narita (NRT)
- Further out, ~60-90 min to central Tokyo
- Train options: Narita Express (N'EX) — direct to Tokyo/Shinjuku/Shibuya, or Keisei Skyliner — faster/cheaper to Ueno
- Historically the main international hub, still used by many airlines (especially budget/some US carriers)
- Airport limousine buses also run to major hotels

### Narita terminals & which station to use
- **Terminal 1** — has its own station, "Narita Airport Terminal 1", right below the terminal (both N'EX/JR and Keisei Skyliner stop here).
- **Terminal 2 / Terminal 3** — share a single station, "Airport Terminal 2・3", also serving both N'EX and Skyliner. Terminal 3 (the low-cost carrier terminal — Jetstar, Spring, Peach, etc.) is a ~5-10 min walk/moving walkway from the T2/3 station, not right beside it, so budget extra time if landing there.
- Airline-to-terminal isn't fixed forever, so double-check on your boarding pass/airline site before travel — but as a rule of thumb: most full-service international carriers use T1, JAL/some alliance partners use T2, and LCCs use T3.
- Ticket counters/vending machines for both N'EX and Skyliner are in the arrivals level of each station; reservations can also be made online in advance.

## To note when booking
- If arrival and departure airports differ (e.g., in HND, out NRT), factor in extra transit time/cost between them (~1-1.5 hrs by train/bus)
- Check flight arrival time vs. last train — trains generally stop around midnight; a late landing may mean a taxi or airport hotel
- IC transit cards (Suica/Pasmo) can be bought at either airport for use on trains/buses/convenience stores throughout the trip
